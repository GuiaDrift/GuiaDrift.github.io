STEM Racing (F1 in Schools)

The world's most exciting STEM competition for students

WHAT IS STEM RACING (F1 IN SCHOOLS)?

STEM Racing (F1 in Schools) is an international STEM competition that challenges students to design, build, and race miniature Formula One cars. It's the only global multi-disciplinary challenge in which teams of students aged 9 to 19 deploy CAD/CAM software to collaborate, design, analyze, manufacture, test, and then race miniature compressed air powered F1 cars.

DESIGN & INNOVATION

Teams use professional CAD software to design aerodynamic cars that can achieve maximum speed on the 20-meter track. Every aspect of the design must comply with detailed technical regulations.

ENGINEERING & MANUFACTURING

The designed cars are manufactured using CNC machines, working with materials like high-density foam. Teams must master precision engineering to create competitive vehicles.

RACING & COMPETITION

Cars are raced on a 20-meter track, powered by CO2 canisters. The competition includes time trials, knockout racing, and reaction time tests, all measured to 1/1000th of a second.

THE COMPETITION PROCESS

STEM Racing (F1 in Schools) follows a comprehensive process that mirrors the real Formula One industry, giving students insight into engineering, design, marketing, and management.

TEAM FORMATION

Students form teams of 3-6 members, assigning roles such as Team Manager, Design Engineer, Manufacturing Engineer, and Marketing Manager.

RESEARCH & DEVELOPMENT

Teams research aerodynamics, materials, and manufacturing techniques to develop their concept car designs.

DESIGN & ANALYSIS

Using CAD software and CFD analysis, teams design their cars and optimize them for performance.

MANUFACTURING

Cars are manufactured using CNC machines with high precision, followed by finishing and assembly.

TESTING

Teams conduct wind tunnel testing, track trials, and performance analysis to refine their designs.

COMPETITION

Teams compete at regional, national, and potentially world finals, presenting their work and racing their cars.
